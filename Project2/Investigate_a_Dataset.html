# Defining a function to avoid reptitive code
def graph_analysis(x1,y1,z1,disease):
    graph=sns.countplot(x=x1,hue=y1, data=z1)
    graph.set_title("Show/NoShow for "+disease+" patients")
    plt.show()

#defining a function for percentage analysis
def percent_analysis(x1,y1,param):
    ax = sns.barplot(x=x1, y=y1)
    ax.set_title("Show percent for "+param)
    plt.ylabel("Percentage of show patients")
    plt.show()

Limitations¶

Though the data has no missing and duplicate values, it would be better if we would be given the time for appointment day as we are given the time when the patient has scheduled the time. The given dataset only takes into account only some of the diseases , it would be better if some more diseases are covered The reason for booking the appointment by the patient should also be column, it would help us get a clearer picture of why or why not a patient didn't turn up for the appointmnent

Conclusions¶

From the above visualizations we can see that female appointments are more than male appointments but actually their turn up ratios to the appointment is almost same. So gender cannot be considered as an important factor in determining who or who will not turn up for the appointment. People with some kind of diseases play an important roe in deciding who or who will not turn up for the appointment. SMS here also plays and important role. People who didn't recieve sms more often tend to turn up for the appointment rather than those who have recieved the appointment sms.
